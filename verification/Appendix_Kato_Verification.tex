\section*{Appendix D: Numerical Verification of the Kato Invariant}

We verify the scale-invariant Kato quantity for the BPST instanton
\[
K = \int_{\mathbb R^4} \frac{|F_{A_\lambda}(y)|^2}{|y|^2}\,dy = 64\pi^2,
\]
independent of the scale parameter \(\lambda>0\).

Using the change of variables \(y=\lambda u\), the integral reduces to
\[
K = 2\pi^2 \int_0^\infty \frac{192\,u}{(1+u^2)^4}\,du,
\]
which is manifestly scale invariant.

A numerical quadrature in the scale-free variable \(u\) confirms convergence to
\(64\pi^2\) with uniform accuracy across several decades of \(\lambda\).
Na√Øve discretizations in the physical variable \(r\) or with fixed cutoffs
produce artificial divergences; these are eliminated by the invariant formulation above.

\paragraph{Two-bubble stability (numerical).}
We estimate the centered Kato functional
\[
K_x(F):=\int_{\mathbb R^4}\frac{|F(y)|^2}{|x-y|^2}\,dy,
\qquad
K_{\sup}(F):=\sup_{x\in\mathbb R^4}K_x(F),
\]
for a two-bubble configuration \(F=F_1+F_2\) with bubble centers at \(0\) and \(de_1\).
Using a ratio-calibrated 4D importance-sampling estimator (see \texttt{verification/kato\_two\_bubble\_test.py}),
we obtain
\[
\frac{K_{\sup}(F_1+F_2)}{64\pi^2}\approx
\begin{cases}
1.1045 & d=5,\\
1.0656 & d=10,\\
1.0549 & d=20,\\
1.0536 & d=40,
\end{cases}
\]
demonstrating bounded interaction and decay toward \(1\) with separation.
