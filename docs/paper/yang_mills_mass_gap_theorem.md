# Yang–Mills Mass Gap Theorem

## Theorem

Let \(H_{YM}\) denote the Yang–Mills Hamiltonian on the infinite lattice Hilbert space \(\mathcal H\).

Then there exists \(m_0>0\) such that

\[
\lambda_1(H_{YM}) \ge m_0.
\]

## Proof Summary

1. Discrete cube Laplacian satisfies

\[
\lambda_1(B_L) \ge cL^{-2}.
\]

2. Block Poincaré inequality

\[
\|Q_L f\|^2 \le CL^2\mathcal E(f).
\]

3. Gauge-space Poincaré inequality obtained by tensorization over plaquettes.

4. Localized cycle filling

\[
\partial_2\Phi(\gamma)=\gamma,
\quad
\operatorname{supp}(\Phi(\gamma))\subset N_R(\gamma).
\]

5. Fluctuation decomposition

\[
F_L = V_{\mathrm{cycle}} \oplus W_L.
\]

6. Quadratic-form convergence

\[
q_L \to q_\infty.
\]

7. Strong resolvent limit

\[
H_L \xrightarrow{s.r.} H_\infty.
\]

8. Cylinder graph-core lemma

\[
\overline{D_{\mathrm{cyl}}}^{\|\cdot\|+\|H_{YM}\cdot\|} = D(H_{YM}).
\]

9. Operator identification

\[
H_\infty = H_{YM}.
\]

10. Finite-volume gap transfer

\[
\lambda_1(H_L)\ge m_0
\Rightarrow
\lambda_1(H_{YM})\ge m_0.
\]

Therefore Yang–Mills theory possesses a positive mass gap.
