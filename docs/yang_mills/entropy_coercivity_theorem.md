# Entropy–Coercivity Reduction for the Yang–Mills Mass Gap

Author: Inacio F. Vasquez
Status: Conditional structural reduction

---

## 1. Physical Hilbert Space

Let H ≥ 0 be the Yang–Mills Hamiltonian acting on the physical Hilbert space ℋ.

Let Ω denote the vacuum.

Define

Q = I − |Ω⟩⟨Ω|

and

ℋ₊ = Ran(Q).

The Yang–Mills mass gap problem is

inf σ(H|ℋ₊) > 0.

---

## 2. Entropy Functional

Let ψ ∈ ℋ with ||ψ|| = 1.

Define Shannon functional

S(ψ) = ∫_{𝒜/𝒢} |ψ(A)|² log |ψ(A)|² dA.

Define entropy rigidity

Φ(ψ) = S(ψ) − S(Ω).

Then

Φ(ψ) ≥ 0

and

Φ(ψ) = 0 ⇔ ψ = Ω.

---

## 3. Entropy Gap Lemma (Required)

There exists c_Φ > 0 such that

Φ(ψ) ≥ c_Φ ||ψ||²

for all ψ ∈ ℋ₊.

Status: Unproven.

---

## 4. Energy–Entropy Coercivity (Required)

There exists C_Φ > 0 such that

⟨ψ, H ψ⟩ ≥ C_Φ Φ(ψ)

for all ψ ∈ Dom(H^{1/2}) ∩ ℋ₊.

Status: Unproven.

---

## 5. Conditional Mass Gap

If the Entropy Gap Lemma and the Energy–Entropy inequality hold then

inf σ(H|ℋ₊) ≥ C_Φ c_Φ > 0.

---

## 6. Interpretation

The Yang–Mills mass gap reduces to establishing an information–energy coercive inequality.

The obstruction is proving that Hamiltonian energy dominates entropy variation in configuration space.
