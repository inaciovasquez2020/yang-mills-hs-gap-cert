# Yang–Mills Mass Gap Research Roadmap

Author: Inacio F. Vasquez
Status: Program roadmap document

---

## 1. Objective

Provide a concrete research roadmap for the rigidity–entropy Yang–Mills mass gap program.

The Clay Millennium problem asks whether the Yang–Mills Hamiltonian

H ≥ 0

has a positive spectral gap

inf σ(H|ℋ₊) > 0.

---

## 2. Program Overview

The rigidity program decomposes the problem into three interacting structural components:

1. Rigidity operator spectral gap
2. Hamiltonian domination inequality
3. Configuration-space compactness

---

## 3. Track A — Rigidity Operator Analysis

Goal: establish

⟨ψ,Rψ⟩ ≥ c_R ||ψ||²

for ψ ∈ ℋ₊.

Key tasks:

• Prove positivity of curvature-distance quadratic form  
• Analyze spectrum of nonlocal rigidity operator  
• Establish kernel structure

---

## 4. Track B — Hamiltonian Domination

Goal:

H ≥ C_R R.

Key tasks:

• Relate curvature differences to magnetic energy  
• Use locality of Yang–Mills Hamiltonian  
• Control nonlocal quadratic forms using local energy bounds

---

## 5. Track C — Configuration Compactness

Goal: prevent arbitrarily small localized excitations.

Key tasks:

• Analyze energy concentration sequences  
• Establish minimal energy packets  
• Control bubbling phenomena

---

## 6. Supporting Analytical Tools

The program likely requires:

Sobolev inequalities on gauge configuration space  
Cluster decomposition estimates  
Spectral theory of nonlocal operators  
Renormalization analysis

---

## 7. Expected Program Flow

Rigidity spectral gap
        ↓
Hamiltonian domination
        ↓
Spectral gap of H
        ↓
Yang–Mills mass gap

Parallel constraint:

Configuration compactness
        ↓
Minimal excitation energy

---

## 8. Long-Term Goal

Integrate operator-theoretic, geometric, and analytic methods into a unified proof of the Yang–Mills mass gap.
