\begin{abstract}
This note imports certified Hilbert--Schmidt and Kato-type obstructions into
the Pulse and Operator--Flow Locality Collapse (OFLC) framework.
The imported results are established by computer-assisted verification
and are used solely as negative inputs constraining admissible locality-based
approaches to the Yang--Mills mass gap problem.
\end{abstract}

limitations of Hilbert--Schmidt and Kato-based coercivity arguments.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Operator--Flow Locality Collapse]
Operator--Flow Locality Collapse (OFLC) refers to the regime in which
operator growth under Heisenberg evolution cannot be bounded by any
cutoff-uniform Lieb--Robinson velocity.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Conditional OFLC Constraint]
Assume the Pulse axiom holds with $\rho(\Lambda)\to\infty$ as
$\Lambda\to\infty$.  
Given the certified failure of Hilbert--Schmidt and Kato-type coercivity,
no locality-based argument relying on such coercivity can establish
a uniform mass gap in the continuum limit.
\end{theorem}

\section{Scope and Status}

All statements in this note are conditional.
Computer-assisted verification is used exclusively to certify
analytic obstructions, not to establish the Yang--Mills mass gap.
Any positive resolution must therefore invoke nonlocal or
non-coercive mechanisms beyond the excluded classes.
