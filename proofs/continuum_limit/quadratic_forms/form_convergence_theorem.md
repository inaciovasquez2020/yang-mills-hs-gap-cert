# Quadratic Form Convergence

Goal

For the renormalized operators

\widetilde H_L = L^2 H_L

prove existence of a dense domain D such that for every f in D,

q_L(f) = <f,\widetilde H_L f> -> q_\infty(f)

and

sup_L q_L(f) < \infty.

Then the Kato form convergence criterion yields

\widetilde H_L -> H_\infty

in strong resolvent sense.

Required structural ingredients

1. Common core D
2. Uniform lower bound q_L(f) >= c ||f||^2
3. Pointwise convergence q_L(f) -> q_\infty(f)
4. Closability of q_\infty
5. Identification H_\infty = H_YM
