# Local Contractibility of the Cube Complex

Each finite lattice cube is contractible as a cell complex.

Consequently, every cycle supported inside a contractible cube is a boundary:

\[
\ker(\partial_1)\cap C_1(\mathrm{cube})
=
\operatorname{im}(\partial_2)\cap C_1(\mathrm{cube}).
\]

This yields local fillings by plaquettes contained in the same cube.

The contractibility constant is uniform over translated cubes of fixed radius.
