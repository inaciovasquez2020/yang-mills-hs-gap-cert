# Hamiltonian Gap Transfer

Assume the finite-volume operator inequality

I - T_L^* T_L >= kappa (-Delta_G)

on the fluctuation complement W_L.

Assume also a finite-volume spectral gap estimate

lambda_1(-Delta_G) >= c L^(-2).

Then

I - T_L^* T_L >= (kappa c) L^(-2)

on W_L.

Therefore the finite-volume coarse operator has a positive spectral gap
m_L = (kappa c) L^(-2).

To obtain an infinite-volume mass gap, one must show:

1. the transfer from coarse fluctuation operator to the finite-volume
   Yang-Mills Hamiltonian preserves positivity;

2. the renormalized lower bound does not collapse under the thermodynamic limit;

3. the physical mass parameter extracted from correlations remains bounded
   below by a positive constant after scale matching.

This reduces the remaining problem to an operator-to-Hamiltonian transfer
principle plus a uniform-in-volume renormalized lower bound.
