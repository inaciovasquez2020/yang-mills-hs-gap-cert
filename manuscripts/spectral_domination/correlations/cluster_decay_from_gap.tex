\section*{Exponential Cluster Decay from Uniform Spectral Gap}

Let $H$ be a self-adjoint Hamiltonian on $\mathcal H$ with
\[
H \ge \gamma (I - P_0),
\quad \gamma > 0,
\]
where $P_0$ is the vacuum projection.

\begin{theorem}[Exponential Decay of Connected Correlations]
Let $A,B$ be bounded local observables with spatial separation $r$.
Then
\[
|\langle \Omega, A B \Omega\rangle
- \langle \Omega, A \Omega\rangle
  \langle \Omega, B \Omega\rangle|
\;\le\;
C\,\|A\|\,\|B\|\, e^{-\gamma r},
\]
where $C$ depends only on locality constants.
\end{theorem}

\textbf{Sketch of Proof.}
Using spectral calculus,
\[
e^{-tH}(I-P_0)
\le e^{-\gamma t}(I-P_0).
\]
For spacelike-separated operators one writes
\[
\langle \Omega, A B \Omega\rangle
-
\langle \Omega, A \Omega\rangle
\langle \Omega, B \Omega\rangle
=
\langle \Omega, A e^{-rH} (I-P_0) B \Omega\rangle.
\]
Applying the spectral bound yields exponential decay in $r$.
\hfill $\square$
